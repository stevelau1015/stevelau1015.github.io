ABSTRACT
A substantial number of audio files and articles regarding music appear online every day. However, research on the complex relationships between different musicians remains inadequate. In this paper, we make use of the context and content of music to analyze the influence of previously produced music on the latest original music. We construct an influence model, which included structural features of networks, to establish associations between a variety of musicians. Then, we apply this model to a case study containing a network of musicians, and investigate the scope of musical influences on a specific band. Finally, we build a matrix of high-level features to measure the similarities between different musicians and use it to distinguish various genres, thus demonstrating the effectiveness and application value of the model we construct.
